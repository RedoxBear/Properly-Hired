Cover Letters: Just How Important Are They?
Source: ResumeGo Research

New research finds that applications with tailored cover letters yield just over 50% more interviews compared to those without.

Research Overview
ResumeGo conducted a two-part study between July 2019 and January 2020 to determine the impact of cover letters on job search success. This included:

Field Experiment: 7,287 fictitious applications were submitted to major job boards like Glassdoor and Indeed.

Professional Survey: 236 recruiters and hiring managers were surveyed regarding their preferences.

Field Experiment Results
Applications were divided into three groups to track interview callback rates within 30 days:

Application Group	Description
Group 1: No Cover Letter	The cover letter field was left blank or filled with "N/A".
Group 2: Generic Letter	Included a well-composed but non-specific summary of qualifications.
Group 3: Tailored Letter	Detailed how the candidate fit the specific company culture and job requirements.
Finding: Applications with tailored cover letters had a 53% higher callback rate than those with no cover letter at all.

Hiring Manager Survey Insights
The survey debunked the common myth that employers do not read cover letters:

Readership: 87% of hiring professionals read cover letters.

Influence: The majority of respondents use cover letters to influence their decision-making.

Tailoring Value: 81% of professionals value tailored letters significantly more than generic ones.

Common Mistakes That Hurt Applicants
According to the survey data, the following issues on a cover letter are most likely to hurt a candidate's chances:

Poorly Written: This was the most significant negative factor, cited by over 70% of respondents.

Using a Generic Letter: Approximately 32% of managers found this detrimental.

Unqualified or Bad Fit: Mentioned by roughly 24% of recruiters.

Unprofessional Formatting: A smaller but still relevant factor at about 6%.

Conclusion
While resumes are critical, submitting a job-specific cover letter has a measurable positive impact on your interview chances. Professionals strongly advise avoiding generic templates and instead focusing on how your skills meet the specific needs of the job description.
