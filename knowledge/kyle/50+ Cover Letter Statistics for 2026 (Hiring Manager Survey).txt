  94% of hiring managers think
  cover letters are essential
       The vast majority of hiring managers (94%) think cover letters
       are influential when deciding who to interview. Hiring managers
       from medium and large companies find cover letters especially
       important, with 34% reporting that they are “very important.”
       According to our findings, 6 in 10 hiring managers (60%) require
       applicants to submit a cover letter. And if you’re applying to a
       medium to large company, the chances are even higher that
       you’ll need one.
       Hiring managers definitely read cover letters — 83% of hiring
       managers read the majority of cover letters they receive. And
       even at companies that don’t require cover letters, 73% of hiring
       managers read them anyway.
       Hiring managers do more than just skim your cover letter, with
       60% spending up to two minutes or more reading cover letters.
       Plus, 45% of hiring managers actually read your cover letter
       before your resume — meaning that cover letters shouldn’t be an
       afterthought.



                 Larger companies receive a higher volume of
                 applications for each open position, so they rely on
                 cover letters to distinguish between candidates with
                 similar qualifications. Cover letters help candidates
                 highlight their unique skills, experiences, and
                 motivations, which may not be obvious from their
                 resumes

  What is the purpose of a cover letter?
  Resume Genius asked hiring managers what they think are the main
  functions of a cover letter. Here’s what our survey findings show:




    <a href="https://resumegenius.com/blog/cover-let…                       Copy HTML



  The Resume Genius survey results
       Across companies of all sizes, hiring managers report that the
       top purpose of a cover letter is to provide additional information
       and context about why a person is applying for the position. This
       finding is especially true at large companies (1000+ employees).
       At small companies (less than 100 employees), the top reason
       hiring managers read cover letters is to determine whether a
       candidate is a good fit for the position.
       At medium-sized companies (101-1000 employees), hiring
       managers say the top purpose of a cover letter is for the
       applicant to show their personality.

  Results from previous surveys
  In 2019, ResumeLab asked 200 hiring managers about the purposes
  of a cover letter:

       63% said explaining your motivation for joining a company is an
       important purpose of a cover letter.
       50% said outlining your career objectives is an important
       purpose of a cover letter.
       50% said explaining why you’re changing careers is an
       important purpose of a cover letter.
       49% said explaining gaps in your employment is an important
       purpose of a cover letter.
       47% said showcasing your achievements is an important
       purpose of a cover letter.

  In 2020, GetCoverLetter surveyed 2,000 recruiters. Of the recruiters
  who prefer that applicants submit cover letters:

       47% said they prefer cover letters because they show an
       applicant is motivated.
       30% said they prefer cover letters because they provide
       additional information about an applicant.
       23% said they prefer cover letters because they show an
       applicant’s personality.

  How important is a cover letter for an internal position?

       47% of those surveyed classified a cover letter for an internal
       position as fairly to very important, compared to 25% who
       classified it as not at all important or only slightly important.
       Hiring managers from medium-sized companies (100-1000
       employees) were more likely than those from small or large
       companies to report a cover letter as fairly or very important
       when hiring for an internal position (62% of hiring managers
       from medium-sized companies compared to 53% and 35% from
       large and small companies, respectively).

  Conclusion
  Overall, the hiring managers we surveyed agree that a persuasive
  cover letter can increase a weak applicant’s chances of getting an
  interview. Our survey also found that cover letters are just as
  important to hiring managers when hiring for internal positions. That

  means every kind of applicant — including career changers, first-
  time job seekers, and internal applicants — should submit a cover
  letter, even if it’s not required.

  A compelling cover letter can be the key to setting yourself apart in a
  crowded applicant pool. To make the strongest impression,
  applicants should always tailor their cover letter by:

       matching the skills in the job posting
       mentioning the company’s goals
       using the company’s name
       addressing the hiring manager by name (when possible)

  Hiring managers consider every part of your cover letter important,
  so ensure each section is carefully planned out. Aim for around 400
  words and begin with an engaging introduction that captures
  attention immediately.

  Finally, format matters: submit your cover letter as a PDF unless
  otherwise specified in the job posting. By customizing and
  presenting your cover letter effectively, you’ll significantly improve
  your chances of standing out and securing an interview.
